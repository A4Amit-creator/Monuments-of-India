    • MONUMENTS IN INDIA

    INDIA GATE

    The India Gate is a war memorial located near the Rajpath on the eastern edge of the "ceremonial axis" of New Delhi.

    TAJ MAHAL

    The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India.

    RED FORT

    The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila is a historic Mughal fort in Delhi, India, that is the primary residence of the Mughal emperors.

    CHAR MINAR

    The Charminar is a monument located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. Constructed in 1591, the landmark is a symbol of Hyderabad

    QUTUB MINAR

    The Qutb Minar, also spelled Qutub Minar and Qutab Minar, is a minaret and victory tower comprising the Qutb complex, which lies at the site of Delhi's oldest fortified city

    HAWA MAHAL

    The Hawa Mahal is a palace in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur

    GOLDEN TEMPLE

    The Golden Temple is a gurdwara located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the pre-eminent spiritual site of Sikhism. It is one of the holiest sites in Sikhism

    JAMA MASJID

    The Masjid-i-Jehan-Numa, commonly known as the Jama Masjid of Delhi, is one of the largest Sunni mosques in India. Its builder is the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan

    VICTORIA MEMORIAL

    The Victoria Memorial is a large marble monument in the Maidan in Central Kolkata, having its entrance on the Queen's Way. It was built between 1906 and 1921 by the British Raj.
